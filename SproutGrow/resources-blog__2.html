Top 5 Gardening Books to Read in 2023

15 min read

September 13th 2023

Blog

Introduction

If you're seeking inspiration, guidance, and wisdom on organic gardening, dive into these top 5 books we recommend for 2023. Covering a spectrum of organic gardening aspects, from planning and designing to self-sufficiency and seed saving, these books offer valuable insights for gardeners of all levels. Let's explore the list!


Top 5 Organic Gardening Books for 2023

  1. The Complete Gardener by Monty Don

    This comprehensive guide covers everything from planning and designing your garden to choosing and growing the right plants for your climate and soil. Learn how to create a beautiful and productive garden in harmony with nature, using organic methods and techniques.

  2. The New Complete Book of Self-Sufficiency by John Seymour

    This classic of self-reliant living teaches you how to grow your own food, make your own clothes, and live off the grid. Explore organic gardening, raising animals, preserving food, making cheese, brewing beer, and more. Get practical advice on coping with challenges such as pests, diseases, and weather.

  3. Organic Gardening for Everyone by CaliKim

    This friendly and personal approach to organic gardening is based on the author's own experience of starting a vegetable garden from scratch. Find step-by-step instructions on growing popular edibles organically and tips on balancing gardening with a busy lifestyle, involving the whole family in the process.

  4. 101 Organic Gardening Hacks by Shawna Coronado

    This collection of clever and creative ideas makes your organic garden more efficient, eco-friendly, and fun. Discover hacks on reusing and recycling materials, saving water and energy, attracting beneficial insects, and improving your soil. Learn how to grow more food in less space using vertical gardening, containers, and raised beds.

  5. Back Garden Seed Saving by Sue Stickland

    This practical guide teaches you how to save seeds from your own garden, preserving genetic diversity and heirloom varieties. Learn how to select, harvest, store, and sow seeds from over 100 common vegetables, herbs, and flowers. Discover how to prevent cross-pollination, test germination, and exchange seeds with other gardeners.


Book Links

Conclusion

Enhance your organic gardening journey with insights from these must-read books. Happy reading and happy gardening!
