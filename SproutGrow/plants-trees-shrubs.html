Trees & Shrubs

Trees and shrubs are woody plants that have a main stem or trunk and branches. They are often grown for their ornamental value, but they also provide shade, privacy, wildlife habitat, and other benefits. Trees and shrubs may be evergreen, keeping their leaves all year round, or deciduous, losing their leaves in the fall. Trees and shrubs are essential for creating a balanced and structured garden that will enhance your home and your environment. Sprout & Grow offers a range of trees and shrubs that are suitable for different sizes, shapes, and styles of gardens. Some examples of trees and shrubs are:

Maple

A deciduous tree that has lobed, palmate leaves and winged seeds. Maple is known for its brilliant fall color, ranging from yellow to orange to red. It also provides sap for making maple syrup. It prefers full sun to partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, and it is tolerant of various conditions. Prune it in the late winter or early spring to remove dead, diseased, or damaged branches and shape the tree. Maple is a great choice for adding a touch of warmth and a sense of season to your garden.

Magnolia

An evergreen or deciduous tree or shrub that has large, fragrant, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, purple, or yellow. Magnolia blooms in the spring or summer, and some varieties have attractive foliage and fruit. It likes full sun to partial shade and moist, acidic, well-drained soil, and it is resistant to most pests and diseases. Prune it in the late winter or early spring to remove dead, diseased, or damaged branches and shape the plant. Avoid pruning in the summer, as it may cause bleeding.

Hydrangea

A deciduous shrub that has large, showy clusters of flowers in shades of white, pink, blue, or purple. Hydrangea blooms from summer to fall, and the flower color may change depending on the soil pH. It likes partial shade and moist, fertile, well-drained soil, and it needs regular watering and mulching. Prune it in the late winter or early spring, depending on the type and flowering time. Cut back the old flowers to encourage new ones.

Azalea

A deciduous or evergreen shrub that has small, oval leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, red, purple, or orange. Azalea blooms in the spring or summer, and some varieties have a pleasant fragrance. It likes partial shade and moist, acidic, well-drained soil, and it needs regular watering and mulching. Prune it after flowering to remove dead, diseased, or damaged branches and shape the plant. Deadhead the faded flowers to improve the appearance and prevent seed formation.

Birch

A deciduous tree that has simple, alternate leaves and catkins. Birch is known for its distinctive bark, which may be white, gray, black, or brown, and may peel or flake. It also provides sap for making birch syrup. It prefers full sun and moist, cool, well-drained soil, and it is tolerant of various conditions. Prune it in the late summer or early fall to remove dead, diseased, or damaged branches and shape the tree. Avoid pruning in the spring, as it may cause bleeding.

Lilac

A deciduous shrub that has heart-shaped leaves and cone-shaped clusters of fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, purple, or blue. Lilac blooms in the late spring or early summer, and some varieties have attractive foliage and fruit. It likes full sun and well-drained soil, and it is resistant to most pests and diseases. Prune it after flowering to remove dead, diseased, or damaged branches and shape the plant. Cut back the old flowers to encourage new ones.
