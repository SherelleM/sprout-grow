Perennials

Perennials are plants that live for more than two years, usually flowering every year after reaching maturity. They are often grown for their long-lasting and varied blooms, but they also provide foliage, fragrance, and texture to the garden. Perennials may die back to the ground in the winter, but they will regrow from their roots in the spring. Perennials are perfect for creating a lasting and diverse garden that will change and evolve over time. Sprout & Grow offers a variety of perennials that are hardy, beautiful, and easy to care for. Some examples of perennials are:

Lavender

A fragrant and beautiful herb that has purple, blue, or white flowers and silvery-green foliage. Lavender is often used for aromatherapy, crafts, and cooking. It likes full sun and well-drained, sandy soil, and it is drought-tolerant and deer-resistant. Prune it lightly in the spring and more heavily after flowering to maintain its shape and vigor. Harvest the flowers when they are partially open and dry them for later use. Lavender is a wonderful choice for adding a soothing scent and a lovely color to your garden.

Hosta

A shade-loving and low-maintenance plant that has large, green, blue, or variegated leaves and spikes of white or purple flowers. Hosta is often grown for its foliage, but it also attracts hummingbirds and butterflies. It prefers moist, rich, well-drained soil, and it is tolerant of most pests and diseases. Divide it every few years to increase its spread and rejuvenate its growth. Cut back the foliage after the first frost and mulch it well for the winter. Hosta is a wonderful choice for adding a lush and cool look to your garden.

Coneflower

A cheerful and hardy flower that has daisy-like petals in shades of purple, pink, white, or yellow and a prominent cone-shaped center. Coneflowers attract butterflies, bees, and birds, and they are often used for medicinal purposes. They like full sun and well-drained soil, and they are drought-tolerant and pest-resistant. Deadhead the faded flowers to prolong the blooming season or leave them for the birds to enjoy. Divide them every three to four years to prevent overcrowding and improve flowering. Coneflower is a wonderful choice for adding a splash of color and a touch of nature to your garden.

Daylily

A colorful and easy-to-grow flower that has trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, pink, red, purple, and more. Daylilies have grass-like foliage and bloom for several weeks in the summer. Each flower lasts only one day, but new ones open every day. They like full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, and they are adaptable to various conditions. Divide them every three to five years to prevent overcrowding and improve flowering. Deadhead the spent flowers to keep them tidy. Daylily is a wonderful choice for adding a vibrant and cheerful touch to your garden.

Peony

A showy and fragrant flower that has large, ruffled petals in shades of white, pink, red, or yellow. Peonies have glossy, green foliage and bloom in late spring or early summer. They make excellent cut flowers and can last for a week or more in a vase. They prefer full sun and rich, well-drained soil, and they are resistant to most pests and diseases. Plant them at the right depth and avoid moving them once established. Stake them if needed to support their heavy blooms. Cut back the foliage after the first frost and mulch them well for the winter. Peony is a wonderful choice for adding a romantic and luxurious feel to your garden.

Sage

A aromatic and edible herb that has gray-green leaves and spikes of purple, blue, pink, or white flowers. Sage is often used for seasoning, tea, and medicine. It likes full sun and well-drained, sandy soil, and it is drought-tolerant and deer-resistant. Prune it in the spring and after flowering to maintain its shape and prevent woody growth. Harvest the leaves as needed and use them fresh or dried. Sage is a wonderful choice for adding a hint of aroma and a dash of taste to your garden and your kitchen.
