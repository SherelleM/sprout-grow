Houseplants

Houseplants are plants that are grown indoors for their ornamental, air-purifying, or therapeutic benefits. They are often chosen for their ability to adapt to low-light, low-humidity, and temperature fluctuations. They also provide foliage, fragrance, and texture to the home. Houseplants may need regular watering, fertilizing, pruning, and repotting, depending on their species and growing conditions. Houseplants are perfect for creating a cozy and inviting atmosphere that will improve your mood and your health. Sprout & Grow offers a variety of houseplants that are easy to care for and beautiful to look at. Some examples of houseplants are:

Snake Plant

A low-maintenance and resilient plant that has upright, sword-shaped leaves with green, yellow, or white stripes. Snake plants are known for their ability to purify the air and remove toxins, such as formaldehyde and benzene. They prefer bright, indirect light and well-drained soil, and they can tolerate drought and neglect. Water them sparingly and let the soil dry out between waterings. Avoid overwatering, as it can cause root rot.

Spider Plant

A popular and easy-to-grow plant that has long, arching leaves with green and white stripes. Spider plants produce baby plants, or spiderettes, that hang from the mother plant and can be propagated easily. They are also effective at filtering the air and removing pollutants, such as carbon monoxide and xylene. They like bright, indirect light and well-drained soil, and they need regular watering and fertilizing. Keep them away from direct sun, as it can scorch their leaves.

Peace Lily

A beautiful and elegant plant that has glossy, dark green leaves and white, spath-like flowers. Peace lilies are one of the few flowering houseplants that can bloom in low-light conditions. They are also excellent at purifying the air and removing toxins, such as ammonia and formaldehyde. They prefer moist, well-drained soil and high humidity, and they need regular watering and fertilizing. Keep them away from cold drafts, as it can damage their leaves and flowers.

Aloe Vera

A succulent and medicinal plant that has fleshy, spiky leaves with gel-like sap. Aloe vera is widely used for its healing and soothing properties, especially for burns, cuts, and skin irritations. It likes bright, indirect light and well-drained, sandy soil, and it is drought-tolerant and easy to care for. Water it deeply and infrequently, and let the soil dry out between waterings. Avoid overwatering, as it can cause root rot.

Jade Plant

A lucky and long-lived plant that has thick, oval-shaped leaves and small, pink or white flowers. Jade plants are considered to bring good fortune and prosperity, according to the Feng Shui principles. They prefer bright, direct light and well-drained soil, and they can store water in their leaves and stems. Water them moderately and let the soil dry out between waterings. Avoid overwatering, as it can cause leaf drop and root rot.

Orchid

A exotic and graceful plant that has slender stems and colorful, butterfly-like flowers. Orchids come in many varieties, shapes, and sizes, and they can bloom for weeks or months. They like bright, indirect light and well-drained, bark-based soil, and they need high humidity and good air circulation. Water them thoroughly and let the excess water drain out. Avoid overwatering, as it can cause root rot and fungal diseases.
