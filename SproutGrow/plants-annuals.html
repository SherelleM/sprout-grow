Annuals

Annuals are plants that complete their life cycle in one growing season, from seed to flower to seed. They are often grown for their colorful and showy blooms, but they also provide foliage, fragrance, and texture to the garden. Annuals are usually removed after they die in the fall or winter, but some may self-seed and come back the next year. Annuals are ideal for adding seasonal variety and interest to your garden, and they can be easily changed and replaced according to your preferences. Some examples of annuals are:

Marigold

A cheerful and easy-to-grow flower that comes in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Marigolds attract beneficial insects and repel pests, making them a good companion plant for vegetables. They prefer full sun and well-drained soil, and they can tolerate drought and heat. Deadhead the spent flowers to encourage more blooming. Marigolds are a great choice for adding a splash of color and a touch of charm to your garden.

Petunia

A popular and versatile flower that can be grown in containers, hanging baskets, or beds. Petunias have a wide range of colors, shapes, and sizes, and some have a pleasant fragrance. They like full sun to partial shade and moist, fertile soil. Pinch back the stems to promote bushy growth and remove faded flowers to prolong the flowering season. Petunias are a wonderful option for creating a stunning display of flowers that will delight your senses.

Cosmos

A delicate and graceful flower that comes in shades of pink, purple, white, and yellow. Cosmos have feathery foliage and daisy-like blooms that sway in the breeze. They are easy to grow from seed and will self-sow in the garden. They like full sun and well-drained soil, and they are drought-tolerant and pest-resistant. Cut the flowers for bouquets or let them go to seed for the birds. Cosmos are a great choice for adding a touch of elegance and a sense of movement to your garden.

Zinnia

A bright and cheerful flower that attracts butterflies and hummingbirds. Zinnias come in many colors, heights, and forms, and they make excellent cut flowers. They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, and they are resistant to deer and rabbits. Water them at the base to avoid fungal diseases and deadhead regularly to keep them blooming. Zinnias are a fantastic choice for adding a burst of color and a bit of whimsy to your garden.

Basil

A fragrant and flavorful herb that is widely used in cooking. Basil has green, purple, or variegated leaves and spikes of white or purple flowers. It grows best in full sun and rich, moist soil, and it needs regular watering and fertilizing. Pinch off the flower buds to prevent bolting and encourage bushy growth. Harvest the leaves as needed and use them fresh or dried. Basil is a great choice for adding a hint of aroma and a dash of taste to your garden and your kitchen.

Sunflower

A striking and cheerful flower that can grow up to 12 feet tall. Sunflowers have large, yellow, orange, or red petals and a dark center that produces edible seeds. They prefer full sun and well-drained soil, and they can tolerate drought and poor soil. Water them deeply and infrequently, and stake them if needed to support their heavy heads. Cut the flowers when they are fully open and enjoy them indoors or leave them for the birds. Sunflowers are a great choice for adding a bit of drama and a lot of joy to your garden.
